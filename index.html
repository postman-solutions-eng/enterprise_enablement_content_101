What is an API?


“API” stands for Application Programming Interface. It enables software to communicate with other software.

Sometimes trickier concepts are more easily understood through analogies, so let’s imagine a digital restaurant in which there are three characters.

  • A client (the patron, or application, at the restaurant)
  • A waiter (the API, or middle service, at the restaurant)
  • A chef (the server that assembles data, or ingredients, and intimately knows the data structures of the database)

In this example, the API offered up a menu to the client from which the client could choose what to consume.
